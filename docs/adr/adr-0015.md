# ADR-0015 — Field-engineer-promisable support boundaries

- **Status**: Accepted
- **Date**: 2026-05-01
- **Deciders**: Field Engineering Lead, PM (V0 planning)
- **Context**: The strategic problem Conduit was created to address is field engineers absorbing unbounded bespoke-agent support load at customer sites. Without a clear support boundary, Conduit recreates the same problem in a new wrapper.
- **Decision**: Conduit is community-supported via GitHub Issues. Honeycomb's commercial support covers data-after-ingest only. Field engineers may engage on customer Conduit issues at strategic-account discretion but make no contractual promises.
- **Alternatives considered**:
  - Full Honeycomb commercial support for Conduit (rejected: would expand Honeycomb's product surface in a way that requires V1+ infrastructure).
  - "Best-effort" without a written boundary (rejected: customer escalations would erode the boundary in practice).
- **Consequences**: Customers know what they get. Field engineers have a written line they can cite in PoCs and read aloud to platform teams. The boundary is reiterated in the field readiness pack. Customers who want commercial Conduit support can either run Honeycomb Collector (which is in commercial scope) or wait for V2+.
